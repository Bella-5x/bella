1.1Revision on relation

A Relation is a set of ordered pairs. Given two sets A and B, a relation from A to B is defined as any subset of A x B.

A relation on A is any subset of AX A. Let R be a relation from A to B. Then,

Domain of R= {xe A: (x, y) = R, for some ye B}

Range of R = {ye B: (x, y) ER, for some xE A}

If R is a relation from A to B, then you may want to know what the inverse of R is. The following definition explains what we mean by the inverse of a relation.

Definition 1.1

Let R be a relation from A to B. The inverse of R, denoted by R¹, is a relation from B to A, given by

R¹ = {(b, a): (a, b) εR}.
